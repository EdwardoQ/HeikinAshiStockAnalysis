        🕯️ What is Heikin Ashi?

        Heikin Ashi (Japanese for "average bar") is a charting technique that uses mathematical averages to smooth out price data, filtering out the daily "noise" and volatility of traditional candlestick charts. It is explicitly designed to make spotting and riding trends incredibly easy.

        • Traditional Candles show the exact Open, High, Low, and Close of a given day. They can rapidly flip red and green, causing traders to panic sell early.
        • Heikin Ashi Candles use the previous day's candle to calculate today's open, and an average of today's price action to calculate the close.
        • The Golden Rule: A green candle with no lower wick means a strong uptrend. A red candle with no upper wick means a strong downtrend.

        📚 Trading Glossary & Concepts

        The "Golden Run"

        A "Golden Run" is a proprietary term in this terminal referring to a massive, sustained uptrend consisting of 5 or more consecutive Green Heikin Ashi candles. When a stock proves it is capable of producing Golden Runs, it means heavy institutional money frequently buys and holds it. The screener looks for stocks that have a history of Golden Runs and are currently starting a brand new reversal.

        The Doji Candle

        A Doji is a candlestick with a very small (or non-existent) body and long wicks on both the top and bottom. It looks like a plus sign (+). In the Heikin Ashi system, a Doji represents complete market indecision and trend exhaustion. If a stock has been dropping for several days and suddenly prints a Doji, it is a high-probability warning that the bottom is in and a bullish reversal is imminent.

        🎯 Common Heikin Ashi Strategies

        • The Trend Rider: Buy on the first solid Green candle that appears after a sequence of Red candles. Hold the stock entirely until the first Red candle appears, taking all emotion out of the trade.
        • The Pullback Entry: Identify a stock in a long-term uptrend (Price > MA20). Wait for a healthy 2 to 3 day red pullback. Buy immediately when it prints a new Green reversal candle.
        • The Wickless Accelerator: Only add to your portfolio position when a green candle prints with absolutely no lower wick. This confirms pure, uninterrupted buying pressure.

        ☁️ Ichimoku Cloud Strategies

        The Ichimoku Cloud (Kumo) is a macro-trend indicator that projects dynamic support and resistance levels into the future. When enabled in Settings, it draws a shaded zone directly on your chart.

        • Price ON TOP OF a Red Cloud: The top boundary of the red cloud acts as a horizontal support floor. This confirms a bullish breakout retest. Buy on a Green HA Bounce.
        • Price UNDER a Green Cloud: The stock is in a macro downtrend. The green cloud above acts as a heavy resistance ceiling. Any green HA candles here are likely fakeout "dead cat bounces." Avoid buying.
        • Price INSIDE the Cloud: The market is in a volatile, choppy consolidation phase. It is a "no trade zone" where trends go to die. Wait for a clear breakout.

        📊 Indicator Definitions

        Moving Averages (MA5 & MA20)

        Tracks the average closing price over the last 5 days (short-term) and 20 days (medium-term).
        ABOVE Price is higher than the average, indicating an uptrend.
        BELOW Price is lower than the average, indicating a downtrend.

        ADX (Average Directional Index)

        Measures the strength of a trend, regardless of whether it is going up or down. A value below 20 means the stock is moving sideways (choppy). A value above 25 means the current trend is strong.
        ADX: 35 High trend strength. Ideal for trend-following entries.

        MACD (Moving Average Convergence Divergence)

        Measures momentum by comparing fast and slow moving averages.
        MACD: BULL The MACD histogram is pointing upward, confirming buying momentum.
        MACD: BEAR The MACD histogram is pointing downward, indicating selling pressure.

        Supertrend

        A volatility-based trend barrier. It calculates the Average True Range (ATR) to determine if a trend is safely intact.
        SUPERTREND: BULL Price is safely above the Supertrend support barrier.
        SUPERTREND: BEAR Price is below the barrier (often means any green candle is just a fakeout).

        Parabolic SAR (PSAR)

        Stop and Reverse dots. PSAR identifies trend direction and potential reversal points.
        PSAR: BULL The PSAR dot is below the price candle, indicating upward acceleration.
